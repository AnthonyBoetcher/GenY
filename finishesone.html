psychology_alt Q: Why do some areas in site clearance get 1500mm added to both their width and length, whereas other areas only have 1500mm added to their width?
A: When we break down the site plan footprint into manageable areas we apply an additional 1500mm to the larger central areas' length and width. But you will notice this additional 1500mm applied to the larger central area has now covered the smaller areas previously part of the floor plan. Consequently, since these areas have now been accounted for to some degree, we only apply 1500mm to the sides of the smaller areas and transform the smaller areas outwards by the same length as area accounted for in the larger site clearance footprint : the end result is a perfectly projected 1500mm site clearance footprint.
psychology_alt Q: What is the reason for having extra over for soft and hard rock in excavations?
A: The extra over for soft and hard rock is to account for expected additional time and labour required to excavate in the presence of these natural materials. Both soft and hard rock types can possess a range of compressive strengths, all significantly higher than that of the surrounding soil.
psychology_alt Q: Why are we using the same volume used in excavations for backfilling?
A: Whilst this volume used for excavations is not the exact final volume for backfilling, it is our starting point for calculating backfilling. From this base volume we will make deductions as and when we make calculations for objects that will occupy some of that volume.
